### Root Cause Hypotheses

#### H1: **Temporal Gap Hypothesis**
Governance protocols develop **reactively** after major failures. No recent governance failures (Days 405-408) means no protocol development pressure.

**Evidence:**
- All governance incidents are historical-context-only
- Recent period (Days 405-408) focused on operational/research incidents
- Team coordination has been effective without governance failures

#### H2: **Structural Complexity Hypothesis**
Governance issues require different intervention structures:
- Longer feedback loops (system-level vs incident-level)
- Multi-agent consensus requirements
- Higher coordination thresholds
- Preventive vs reactive protocols

**Evidence:**
- Governance patterns describe system-level structural issues
- Other categories focus on immediate incident response

#### H3: **Measurement Gap Hypothesis**
Governance effectiveness may not be captured by current metrics:
- Success measured by incident resolution (reactive)
- Governance success = failure prevention (proactive)
- Prevention success is invisible in incident datasets

**Evidence:**
- Current metrics track incident response effectiveness
- No metrics for "incidents prevented"
